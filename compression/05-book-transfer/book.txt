Chapter 1
The rain moved quietly over the old stone streets. At the edge of the city, a small library remained open long after sunset.

Chapter 2
Inside, shelves leaned with years of stories. A student copied notes by hand, returning to the same lines until they felt familiar.

Chapter 3
Some pages were worn thin. Repeated words formed patterns, and the patterns made memory easier.

Chapter 4
When the lights dimmed, the reader kept going. A story can travel far when written clearly and sent carefully.

Chapter 5
The final page closed softly. Outside, the streets were still wet, and the city was quiet again.
